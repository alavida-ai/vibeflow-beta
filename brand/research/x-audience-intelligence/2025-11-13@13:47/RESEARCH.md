# X Competitive Research: @boringmarketer Audience Intelligence

## Executive Summary

@boringmarketer (87K followers) operates in the same strategic territory as vibeflow: practitioners who use AI for work, build systems, and reject easy-button SaaS. This analysis reveals what resonates with marketing strategists, builders, and entrepreneurs adopting AI infrastructure. The audience data shows clear strategic differentiation opportunities for vibeflow positioning.

---

## Key Finding: Saves > Likes (The Engagement Pattern That Matters)

### The Signal
High-performing tweets consistently get **1.5-2.2x more saves than likes**, revealing audience psychology:

**Top Performers by Saves:**
1. "This prompt sequence" tweet → **1,672 saves** | 691 likes (2.4x ratio)
2. "How to turn website into revenue machine" → **2,223 saves** | 1,049 likes (2.1x ratio)
3. "sooo many ways to use a workflow" → **1,174 saves** | 672 likes (1.7x ratio)
4. "15 Claude Code habits" → **1,616 saves** | 859 likes (1.9x ratio)
5. "Redesign your funnel for conversion" → **536 saves** | 286 likes (1.9x ratio)

### What This Reveals
**NOT entertainment/validation seeking** → They don't want dopamine hits through likes
**Knowledge capture** → They want to reference and apply later
**Implementable value** → Content trusted enough to save for execution
**Utility-first mindset** → Practical frameworks trump emotional resonance

**Implication for Vibeflow:** Create saveable frameworks and systems-thinking content, not motivational inspiration.

### TYPE 1: TACTICAL FRAMEWORKS WITH NUMBERS (HIGHEST ENGAGEMENT)

**Pattern:** Numbered lists, specific metrics, actionable steps, with proof/evidence

**Top Performers:**

| Tweet | Type | Likes | Saves | Ratio | Views |

**Characteristics:**

**Engagement Driver:** Saves indicate audience wants to reference and apply. High saves = knowledge capital they value.

- "Steal these 15 habits"

---

### Casualness: 75-80% Casual
**Examples:**
- "it cracks me up when AI gets hyped"
- "let's vibe!!"
- "Anthropic cooked!"
- "so many incredible marketing use cases"
- "You'll just launch ad campaigns on GPT with couple prompts eventually"

**Pattern:** Professional but conversational, uses contractions, personality-forward without being frivolous.

---

### Specificity: Obsessive About Numbers
**Examples:**
- "2,847 members" not "thousands"
- "$400/week to $15" not "save money"
- "90% handled by TWO" not "fewer tools"
- "60%+ of traffic is mobile" not "most traffic"

**Pattern:** Every metric quantified. Vague claims avoid; specific data dominates.

---

### Evidence-Based: Always Shows Work
**Methods Used:**
- Screenshots of prompts/results
- Video tutorials showing actual implementation
- Real metrics from real businesses
- Specific tool names (Claude Code, Perplexity, Firecrawl, n8n)
- Real customer quotes and results

**Pattern:** Claims always backed by evidence. Audience trusts because they can verify.

---

### Anti-Hype: Actively Rejects Marketing Speak
**Examples:**
- "Boring beats shiny"
- "Specific beats vague"
- "Numbers create believability"
- "it cracks me up when AI gets hyped"
- "Real testimonials/facts only"

**Pattern:** Credibility built through rejection of marketing clichés, not adoption of them.

## Content Format Success Patterns

### What Drives 1,000+ Saves
1. **Numbered frameworks with numbers** (15 habits, 10 takeaways)
2. **Before/after metrics** (transformation clarity)
3. **Step-by-step implementation** (video + written)
4. **Real examples from real work** (screenshots, data)

### What Drives Discussion (Replies)
1. **Contrarian takes** (challenge status quo)
2. **Questions** (ask audience to engage)
3. **Industry critique** (expose problems)
4. **Controversial positioning** (polarize intentionally)

### What Underperforms
1. Pure philosophy (no tactical application)
2. Generic inspiration (no specificity)
3. Hype language (no evidence)
4. Vague claims (no numbers)
