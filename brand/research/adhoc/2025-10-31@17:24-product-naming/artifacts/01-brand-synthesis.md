## Section 1: Strategic Positioning (Non-Negotiable Framework)

### Category Ownership: "Marketing Architecture"

[FACT] "Marketing Architecture" is completely unclaimed white space with 0% competitor usage
- Source: "0/18 competitors use 'architect,' 'architecture,' or 'engineer' terminology" - `/brand/strategy/positioning/STRATEGY.md`
- Validation: Category analysis across 18 competitors confirmed zero usage

[STRATEGIC ASSUMPTION] A name that signals "architecture," "infrastructure," or "systems" reinforces category ownership
- Basis: All positioning documents consistently emphasize architect/builder identity over user/consumer framing
- Gap: No competitive naming analysis conducted yet—unknown if competitors use architecture-adjacent naming

**Naming Implication:** The name should feel like infrastructure/systems/architecture, NOT consumer SaaS or convenience tools

---

### Positioning Tensions (Core Differentiators)

The brand occupies three specific strategic territories through deliberate contrasts:

**1. Capability Over Convenience**
[FACT] "85% of competitors position on ease language ('effortlessly simple,' 'no coding required')" - `/brand/strategy/brand-fundamentals/STRATEGY.md`
- Our stance: "We build for empowerment, not dependency"
- Naming constraint: Must NOT signal ease, simplicity, or accessibility

**2. Ownership Over Subscription**
[FACT] "100% of competitors operate SaaS subscription models" - `/brand/strategy/positioning/STRATEGY.md`
[FACT] "0% mention ownership or vendor independence" - `/brand/strategy/positioning/STRATEGY.md`
- Our stance: "Own Your Stack, Own Your Destiny"
- Naming implication: Should feel permanent, owned, foundational—NOT rented or transactional

**3. Augmentation Over Replacement**
[FACT] "0% of competitors authentically position on 'AI makes YOU better' vs 'AI does it for you'" - `/brand/strategy/brand-fundamentals/STRATEGY.md`
- Our stance: "Enhance Humans, Don't Replace Them"
- Naming constraint: Must NOT signal automation, replacement, or autonomy

## Section 10: Strategic Questions for Name Evaluation

When evaluating candidate names, ask:

### Identity Alignment
1. Does this name help someone become a "Marketing Architect"?
2. Would a sophisticated marketing strategist be proud to tell peers they use this?
3. Does this name signal mastery over convenience?

### Category Ownership
1. Does this reinforce "Marketing Architecture" as a category?
2. Could competitors using SaaS subscription models claim this name authentically?
3. Does this feel like infrastructure or like a tool?

### Competitive Differentiation
1. Is this name defensibly different from competitors?
2. Does it occupy white space or crowded territory?
3. Does it require us to compromise our strategic constraints?

### Cultural Authenticity
1. Does this honor Spanish-American cultural roots authentically?
2. Is it appropriative or tokenistic?
3. Does it work seamlessly across English and Spanish markets?

### Practical Viability
1. Is the domain available?
2. Is it pronounceable and memorable?
3. Does it scale beyond the initial product offering?
