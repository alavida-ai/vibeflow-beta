## Category 1: Positioning & Differentiation

### 1. Obviously Awesome by April Dunford (2019)

**Focus:** Product positioning for B2B tech companies

**Core Framework:** 10-step positioning process to clarify value proposition

**Target Audience:** B2B tech companies, early-stage startups

**Length:** ~200 pages

**Key Promise:** Find your product's "secret sauce" and communicate it clearly

**Sources:**
- https://www.amazon.com/Obviously-Awesome-Product-Positioning-Customers/dp/1999023005
- https://sobrief.com/books/obviously-awesome
- https://durmonski.com/book-summaries/obviously-awesome/

## Selection Criteria Reminder

Based on Alavida's context, books will be scored on:

1. **Relevance to B2B client acquisition** (30%) - Must directly address landing enterprise/B2B clients
2. **Actionability** (25%) - Practical frameworks, not abstract theory
3. **Community/content focus** (20%) - Covers social media, community building, content strategy
4. **Stage appropriateness** (15%) - Suitable for first marketing campaign (not scaled operations)
5. **Time-to-value** (10%) - Can be read and applied quickly
