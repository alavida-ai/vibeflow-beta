**Avoid posting:**

**Long Tutorials:**
- Setup walkthroughs too complex for X
- Multi-step processes requiring sustained attention
- Technical deep-dives needing pausing and following along
- **Strategy:** Drive these viewers straight to YouTube, don't attempt to summarize

**Building in Public Raw Footage:**
- Episodic content requiring previous context
- Behind-scenes that's interesting only to subscribers
- Community updates for existing fans
- **Strategy:** Short highlights for community, full episodes YouTube-only

### Step 3: Package for X Algorithm

X algorithm has specific preferences. Optimize for discovery and engagement.

#### Captions/Subtitles (REQUIRED)

**80% of X video watched without sound.** Captions aren't optional.

**How to add captions:**

**Option 1: Burned-in subtitles (recommended)**
- Add subtitles directly in video editing software
- Use large, readable font (impact bold or similar)
- White text with black outline (readable on any background)
- Position in lower third (doesn't block interface)
- **Advantage:** Works for all viewers automatically

**Option 2: X auto-captions**
- Upload video, X generates captions automatically
- Review and edit (auto-captions often wrong for technical terms)
- **Advantage:** Easier workflow, but quality inconsistent

**Caption style guidelines:**
- Keep sentences short (3-5 words per caption card)
- Break at natural pauses (not mid-word or mid-phrase)
- Use sentence case (not ALL CAPSâ€”harder to read)
- Highlight key terms if helpful ("Claude Code," "Multi-Agent," etc.)

#### Aspect Ratio Selection

**Choose based on content type:**

**16:9 (Landscape) - Use for:**
- Screen recordings showing Claude Code interface
- System demonstrations requiring wide view
- Side-by-side comparisons
- Tutorial clips
- **Advantage:** Shows full interface clearly, professional aesthetic

## Part 4: Brand Voice Consistency

X content must maintain Marketing Architecture positioning while adapting to platform norms.

---

### Technical Credibility (Show Don't Tell)

**On X, proof over claims.**

**Do:**
- Share actual system outputs (screenshots of generated content, research briefs)
- Show interface in action (screen recordings of workflows)
- Post real metrics (time savings, quality improvements)
- **Example:** "This system analyzed 50 articles in 28 minutes. Manual research: 6+ hours. Here's the output: [screenshot]"

**Don't:**
- Make unsubstantiated claims ("Revolutionary AI system!")
- Hype without proof ("You won't believe what this does!")
- Generic promises ("10x your productivity!")

---

### Ownership Philosophy (Build > Rent Language)

**Consistently reinforce infrastructure thinking.**

**Do:**
- Use "build," "architect," "design," "own" verbs
- Frame as systems not tools: "marketing infrastructure" not "marketing hacks"
- Emphasize equity building: "This builds value in your business, not someone else's"
- **Example:** "Your marketing stack should be code you ownâ€”not SaaS subscriptions that own you."

**Don't:**
- Use "use," "try," "leverage" (passive tool language)
- Frame as convenience: "easy," "simple," "effortless"
- Promise automation without human judgment: "AI does it for you"

---

### Evidence-Based Approach (Show Receipts)

**Marketing Architecture brand built on proof, not hype.**

**Do:**
- Include data in claims (time saved, quality metrics, cost comparisons)
- Show process (not just results)
- Acknowledge limitations ("This works for X, but not Y")
- **Example:** "Spent 6 hours building this workflow. Now it saves 4 hours per week. ROI: 3 weeks. Here's how: [link]"

**Don't:**
- Exaggerate results (unrealistic time savings, universal solutions)
- Hide failures (transparency builds trust)
- Make absolute claims ("Never do X again!")

---

### Systems Thinking (Not Just Tactics)

**Differentiate through intellectual depth.**

**Do:**
- Explain "why" not just "how": "This approach works because [principle]"
- Connect tactics to strategy: "This workflow reflects systems thinking: [insight]"
- Teach frameworks: "Here's the decision framework I use: [diagram]"
- **Example:** "Most marketers automate tasks. Marketing Architects design systems. The difference: [thread explaining]"

**Don't:**
- Post pure tactics without context ("Do X to get Y")
- Focus on tools over thinking ("Just use this tool!")
- Skip the reasoning ("Here's what to do, trust me")

---

### Voice Consistency Checklist

Before posting X content, verify:

- [ ] Uses "build/architect/design" language (not "use/try")
- [ ] Includes proof or evidence (screenshots, data, examples)
- [ ] Avoids hype or exaggeration (no "revolutionary," "insane," "mind-blowing")
- [ ] Maintains ownership philosophy (infrastructure > tools, equity > subscriptions)
- [ ] Shows technical credibility (real systems, not vague promises)
- [ ] Teaches frameworks or principles (not just tactics)
- [ ] Active voice throughout (not passive constructions)
- [ ] Authentic tone (conversational, not corporate)
