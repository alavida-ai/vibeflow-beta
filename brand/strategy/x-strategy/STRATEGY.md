# X Strategy: Cross-Platform Amplification

**Core Philosophy:** YouTube = depth infrastructure you own. X = discovery distribution you leverage. Content flows YouTube → X, never reverse.

---

## What Makes Content Engaging on X (Evidence-Based)

### Finding #1: Saves > Likes = Knowledge Capture Mindset
**Pattern:** Top content gets **1.5-2.2x more saves than likes**

**What This Means:**
- Your audience captures knowledge for later reference (not entertainment)
- They want implementable frameworks, not inspiration
- Evidence-based claims with specific numbers drive saves

**For You:** Create saveable content—frameworks, workflows, systems

---

### Finding #2: Tactical Frameworks Win Massively
**High-engagement formats:**
- Numbered frameworks ("15 habits," "10 takeaways")
- Before/after metrics ("$400/week → $15")
- Step-by-step breakdowns with evidence
- Screenshots showing real systems/results

**For You:** Extract tactical snippets from YouTube tutorials—the "aha moments" that are self-contained and actionable

---

### Finding #3: Specificity Over Hype
**What works:** "$400/week to $15" | "2,847 members" | "90% handled by 2 tools"
**What fails:** "save money" | "thousands" | "fewer tools"

**For You:** Always use concrete numbers, metrics, timeframes

---

### Finding #4: Contrarian Takes Drive Discussion
**Pattern:** Provocative statements get **high replies** (37-83 replies/post)

**Examples:**
- "You won't need UIs like this"
- "Tell me how X differs from Y"
- Challenge industry narratives

**For You:** Turn YouTube contrarian positioning into debate-starters
