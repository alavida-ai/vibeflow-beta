# Title Tactics: Power Words, Formulas, and Optimization

## Power Word Directory

### Exclusivity Words
- Secret, Hidden, Underrated, Underground, Exclusive, Only, Rare, Banned, Forbidden, Leaked, Unknown, Insider

### Urgency Words  
- Before, Last, Final, Ending, Closing, Deadline, Urgent, Hurry, Limited, Soon, Now, Finally

### Emotion Words
- Shocking, Incredible, Unbelievable, Jaw-dropping, Beautiful, Heartbreaking, Thrilling, Fascinating, Surprising, Unexpected

### Authority Words
- Expert, Pro, Official, Science, Research, Study, Data, Proven, Academy, Master, Certified

### Curiosity Words
- Why, How, What, Reveal, Secret, Discovery, Truth, Exposed, Mystery, Question

## Title Formula Library

### Educational
- "How to [achieve goal] in [timeframe]"
- "The [number] ways to [achieve goal]"
- "[Expert] explains [complex topic]"
- "What nobody tells you about [topic]"

### Entertainment  
- "[Reaction] to [popular video/event]"
- "I tested [product comparison]"
- "The [superlative] [subject] ever"

### Story/Documentary
- "Inside [exclusive access]"
- "The story of [person/event]"  
- "[Celebrity/person] reveals [truth]"

### Comparison
- "$[amount] vs $[amount] [product]"
- "[Option A] vs [Option B]"
- "I ranked [number] [items]"

### Listicle
- "[Number] ways to [achieve goal]"
- "[Number] mistakes people make with [topic]"
- "[Number] things [expert] taught me"

## Character Optimization

### Sweet Spot: 50-60 Characters
- Displays fully on desktop
- Displays fully on mobile notifications
- Forces clarity (no padding)
- Memorable length

**Lengths by performance:**
- <45 chars: Very short, punchy
- 50-60 chars: Balanced, optimal
- 70+ chars: Gets cut off, loses effectiveness

## Punctuation Strategy

### Effective Punctuation
- **Parentheses:** "(Secret revealed)" - adds intrigue
- **Brackets:** "[THIS WILL SHOCK YOU]" - emphasis
- **Colons:** "The Secret: How I Made $1M" - structure
- **Question marks:** "Did you know...?" - curiosity
- **Ellipsis:** "And what happened next..." - cliffhanger

### Avoid Overuse
- Too many punctuation marks = unprofessional
- 1-2 punctuation marks optimal
- Varies by niche (education vs entertainment)

## Word Choice Optimization

### Strong Verbs
- Replaced "watch" with "see," "discover," "learn"
- Action-oriented language
- Conveys value/movement

### Avoid Weak Modifiers
- "Really," "Very," "Sort of" = weak
- Use specifics instead (numbers, facts)

### Leverage Numbers
- Numbers = specific, concrete
- "$1M" > "A lot of money"
- "10 minutes" > "Not long"

## Clickbait vs Honest Curiosity

### Crossing the Line (Clickbait - Avoid)
- Title promises something video doesn't deliver
- Misleading expectations
- Damages trust and retention

### Honest Curiosity (Do This)
- Creates gap without misleading
- Video satisfies the promise
- Viewers feel informed and satisfied

**Example:**
- ❌ "This will BLOW YOUR MIND" (over-promise)
- ✅ "How I grew my channel to 1M subs" (honest promise)

## Title Variation Process

### Step 1: Brain Dump (Initial Ideas)
- Write 3-5 first instinct titles
- Don't judge, just create

### Step 2: Strategic Variations
- Vary focus (different aspect of video)
- Vary format (question, statement, list)
- Vary emotional angle (practical, exciting, surprising)
- Vary power words (exclusivity, urgency, emotion)

### Step 3: Selection
- Score each for curiosity, clarity, clickability
- Pick top candidate
- Test if time allows (A/B test)

## Testing and Iteration

### Measurement
- Track CTR for each title
- Compare across videos
- Note which formulas work for your niche

### Refinement
- Double down on working patterns
- Avoid underperforming formulas
- Develop personal style

---

**Core:** 30-40 variations reveal best angle. Power words drive curiosity. 50-60 characters optimal. Test and iterate to find your winning formula.
